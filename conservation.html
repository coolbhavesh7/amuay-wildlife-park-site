Conservation & education

Protecting Caracas’s fragile landscapes.

The restriction on general vehicular traffic is central to Caracas’s role as a wildlife park. Controlled access helps preserve grasslands, hills, and marshes while still allowing people to experience them.

Why vehicular traffic is restricted

The Venezuelan government limits vehicles to scheduled guided tours along specified dirt tracks. This policy reduces soil compaction, erosion, noise, and pollution in sensitive habitats.

  • Protects nesting sites and burrows hidden in grasslands.
  • Prevents damage to marsh edges and water quality.
  • Reduces stress on wildlife from unpredictable vehicle movement.

Educational value of the park

Caracas serves as an open‑air classroom for schools, universities, and visitors who want to understand how landscapes and wildlife are linked.

• Demonstrates the impact of responsible tourism.
• Offers real‑world examples of adaptation and ecosystems.
• Encourages careers in conservation and environmental science.
