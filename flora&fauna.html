Wildlife guide

Animals of the Caracas grasslands, hills, and marshes.

From wading birds to grazing mammals, Caracas Wildlife Park shelters a wide range of species. Use this guide to understand where each group is most likely to be seen along official tour routes.

Key wildlife groups

Grassland mammals

Open grasslands support grazing deer, small rodents, and predators that follow their movements. Look for tracks near the edges of dirt tracks where animals cross at dawn and dusk.

Likely sightings: Deer, foxes Best time: Dusk & dawn

Birdlife of the marshes

Wetlands

Herons, egrets, and other wading birds feed in the shallows, while ducks and other waterfowl shelter in deeper pools. Binoculars are recommended for early‑morning tours.

Likely sightings: Herons, waterfowl Best time: Sunrise

Hillside communities

Hills

Shrub‑covered slopes host songbirds, lizards, and small mammals that rely on the hills’ patchwork of sun and shade. Some viewpoints along tour routes overlook these micro‑habitats.

Likely sightings: Songbirds, lizards Best time: Mid‑morning

How this page is useful

This wildlife overview helps visitors and students connect each landscape in Caracas to the animals that depend on it. By knowing where species are most likely to appear, you can choose tours that match your interests—while still respecting the park’s strict protection rules.

  • Match tour routes to wildlife you want to observe.
  • Prepare checklists for field notes or class projects.
  • Understand why certain tracks are off‑limits to private vehicles.

Wildlife viewing tips

All wildlife in Caracas is protected. Guides ensure vehicles stay on designated dirt tracks and that animals are given space to move freely through their habitats.

• Keep noise low and remain inside vehicles.
• Use binoculars instead of approaching animals.
• Follow your guide’s instructions at all times.
• Never feed wildlife or leave litter behind.
