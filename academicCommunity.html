For Teachers & students

Turn your visit into a strong project.

Use this page as a calm, distraction‑free starting point for your research on Caracas’s wildlife, flora, and conservation rules.

Study focus areas

Instead of trying to record everything, choose one clear question for your project. The other pages on this site give you the background you need to answer it. Visit the Wildlife, Flora, Visit, and Conservation pages and understand what you will see and why access is controlled.

  • How do marsh plants help keep water clean?
  • Which animals are easiest to spot on grasslands vs hills?
  • Why does the park limit vehicles to guided tours?
  • Set one or two clear learning goals per group.
  • Agree on behaviour rules that match park guidelines.
  • Prepare notebooks or checklists for quiet observation.
  • Choose one habitat (grassland, hill, or marsh) to focus on.
  • Record three animals and three plants you can identify or describe.
  • Write one paragraph on how rules (guided tours, no private cars) protect what you saw.

Access rules

  • Only official guided vehicles may use the dirt tracks.
  • No walking away from vehicles without the guide’s permission.
  • No littering, loud music, or shouting near wildlife.

Why these rules matter

They reduce stress on animals, keep visitors safe, and prevent damage to the plants and soils that make Caracas special.
