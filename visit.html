Visitor information

Plan a safe, low‑impact visit to Caracas.

Vehicular traffic in Caracas Wildlife Park is prohibited except for official guided tours along specified dirt tracks. This page helps you understand how to join these tours and what to expect.

Why this page is useful

Planning ahead ensures your visit supports conservation rather than harming it. By learning access rules, recommended times, and what to bring, you arrive prepared and help guides keep the reserve safe for everyone.

  • Clarifies why private vehicles are not allowed inside the reserve.
  • Explains how guided tours protect both visitors and wildlife.
  • Helps you choose tour options based on habitats and interests.

Essential reminders

All visitors must respect official signage and the instructions of park staff. Staying on designated tracks prevents damage to fragile soils, plants, and nesting sites.

• No off‑road driving at any time.
• Follow waste‑disposal guidelines.
• Keep a safe distance from wildlife.
• Report any hazards to your guide.
Guided tour overview

The schedule below is a sample of how Caracas might organise tours to highlight different landscapes while controlling traffic.

Sample itinerary
Sunrise Marsh Tour
Early‑morning departure along marsh‑edge tracks for birdwatching and quiet observation of wetlands as the sun rises.
Plains & Hills Circuit
Mid‑morning route across grasslands and up gentle hills, focusing on grazing mammals, panoramic views, and plant communities.
Evening Wildlife Drive
Late‑afternoon circuit designed to catch the change from day to night, when many animals become more active.
Group size
Small groups per vehicle to reduce noise and allow guides to answer questions and point out wildlife safely.
What to bring

These items improve your comfort and help you observe wildlife without causing disturbance.

Binoculars or camera with zoom Light, long‑sleeved clothing Sun protection (hat, sunscreen) Refillable water bottle Field notebook or checklist
